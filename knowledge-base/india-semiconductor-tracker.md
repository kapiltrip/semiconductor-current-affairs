# India Semiconductor Tracker

Track India separately because the opportunity and timeline are different from the global leading-edge race.

## Why India Matters

India has a strong electronics demand base and a large chip-design talent pool. The strategic gap is manufacturing depth: fabs, compound semiconductors, OSAT/ATMP, materials, equipment, skilled operators, and supplier ecosystems.

## June 2026 Official Programme Snapshot

PIB reported 12 approved semiconductor manufacturing projects with an investment pipeline of approximately ₹1.64 lakh crore: one semiconductor fabrication unit, two compound-semiconductor fabrication units, and nine packaging units. It also reported 24 supported design projects, EDA-tool access for 105 companies, and 23 completed tape-outs.

Treat these as programme and pipeline indicators. An approval is not an operating facility, a tape-out is not a qualified commercial chip, and an announced investment is not expenditure already completed. Track each project through construction, tool installation, first silicon, yield learning, customer qualification, and production volume.

## Career-Relevant Interpretation

Near-term India opportunities are likely to be strongest in:

- RTL design and verification
- DFT
- Physical design and STA
- Analog and mixed-signal design
- Embedded systems and firmware
- EDA flows and methodology
- Test engineering
- Packaging and reliability
- Power electronics and compound semiconductors
- Manufacturing operations and equipment support

## Questions To Be Ready For

1. Why is India starting with OSAT/ATMP and compound semiconductors instead of immediately competing at 2nm?
2. What is the difference between semiconductor design, wafer fabrication, and packaging?
3. Why do fabs need a deep ecosystem of chemicals, gases, wafers, equipment, water, power, and trained operators?
4. How can India use its design talent to move up the semiconductor value chain?
